Joseph Brodsky

The man who very liked his chair and walls

Poet, translator, prose writer, playwright and just a good guy.
Here's his way:
  • In 1940 – Born in Leningrad
  • In 1955 – Brodsky began writing his own poetry and producing literary translations
  • By 1958 – He was already well known in literary circles for his poems "The Jewish cemetery near Leningrad" and "Pilgrims".
  • In 1960 – The young Brodsky met Anna Akhmatova, one of the leading poets of the silver age.
  • In 1963 – Brodsky's poetry was denounced by a Leningrad newspaper as "pornographic and anti-Soviet". During juThe trial judge asked:
    "Who has recognized you as a poet? Who has enrolled you in the ranks of poets?"

    Brodsky replied

    "No one,Who enrolled me in the ranks of the human race?"
    Brodsky was not yet 24.
    For his "parasitism" Brodsky was sentenced to five years hard labor and served 18 months on a farm in the village of Norenskaya, in the Archangelsk region, 350 miles from Leningrad.
  • In 1965 – Brodsky's sentence was commuted after protests by prominent Soviet and foreign cultural figures, including Evgeny Evtushenko, Dmitri Shostakovich, and Jean-Paul Sartre as well as Akhmatova.
  • In December 1965 – Brodsky returned to Leningrad and continued to write over the next seven years, many of his works being translated into German, French and English and published abroad.
  • 1973–1974 – Brodsky went on to become a Visiting Professor at Queens College, Smith College, Columbia University, and Cambridge University, later returning to the University of Michigan (1974–80).
  • In 1987 – he won the Nobel Prize for Literature, the fifth Russian-born writer to do so.
  • In 1991 – Brodsky became Poet Laureate of the United States.
  • On January 1996 – Brodsky died of a heart attack aged 55, in his New York City apartment .
